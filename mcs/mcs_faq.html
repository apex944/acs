    ACS Suspension Technology

    Apex Competition Systems has experience working with suspension technology across a wide range of road racing applications, and we've compiled that knowledge here to address the most common questions around installation, setups, and effective tuning strategies.

    The information below is intended to help you make informed adjustments as your setup evolves. If your specific application or goals go beyond what's here, contact us and we'll provide guidance based on your car, configuration, and use case.

    Damper Design FAQ

    Monotube Construction allows more oil capacity and better heat dissipation than twin tube dampers. The large main piston gives quick, accurate damper response and the divider piston separates oil and gas, eliminating cavitation, allowing for more consistent damping under heavy use.


    Monotubes provide more precise control of compression and rebound forces. This results in better tire contact, improved platform control, and a wider usable tuning range.

    Blow-off valves are extremely effective in managing high speed compression hits where shaft velocities can create significant increases in compression damping forces. The proprietary blow off mechanisms are entirely unique to MCS and minimize a compression strike otherwise applied to a tire.


    In simple terms, the blow-off will allow drivers to aggressively attack curbing without upsetting the chassis.

    Orifices allow for precise hydraulic control of damper oil through the main piston. Utilizing orifices introduces the ability to have a wide operating range, avoiding pesky re-valves, consistency in damping adjustment per click, and virtually eliminates fatigue with usage.

    Motorsport doesn’t forgive compromise. From road racing and endurance to autocross, hill climbs, tarmac rally, and stage rally, MCS dampers deliver mechanical grip and confidence you can feel immediately. Every adjustment is analog — a precise suspension you can dial in by hand, right at the car.
